E N T R Y  N O .  6

"Why do we need to eat rice?"

    If someone would ask me, what is my much-loved food? Perhaps, it would probably take us infinite to finish answering that certain issue. For me, it’s a lot of different when you are discussing about something you preferred by, towards something that you couldn’t live without. It’s because in my perspective, even though if it is your favorite, you could still continue to live without having it. For example, one of my favorite foods is mangoes however I can endure a year without consuming it. 

    Every morning, my mom would always tell me that “kanin ang kainin mo dahil aalis ka”, that even though I am running late already in class, she would still pack it and put it in my bag for later. I am used on having rice as part of my breakfast, lunch, dinner, or even an afternoon-snack. So I therefore conclude that, the food that I can’t live without is rice. During the four-month vacation, I tried to lose weight and removed rice for dinner by replacing it with some light-weight snacks but I only lasted for some weeks. So I decided to lessen my rice intake rather than removing it. 

    According to the Wikipedia, rice is the most essential grain that contributes to one’s sustenance. That makes me think that, maybe it is the main reason on why it makes me and my family healthy and strong. Eating rice as breakfast makes me well-conditioned to fight the day! Specifically with my schedule in school, I can basically cope up with it because I had a wholesome breakfast. It is also the reason on why even though there is an unexpected make-up class or activity, I still have energy for it. 

    Since then, we, Filipinos, are used of having rice as part of their meals every day. For me, my day wouldn’t be complete without having this food. Usually, my favorite food to partner with it is my mom’s ultimate sinigang. Its hot warmth blended with the sour soup of the sinigang would always be my favorite combination. It is my own version of, “walang makakatapat, at babalik-balikan”. 

    It may certainly sound weird for somebody who would hear this revelation of the food that I can’t live without, but I can’t really imagine myself without it for years. Also, it is also a little bit weird to be without it because I am already used to it. Rice as part of a meal makes me strong and well that helps me to fight through the day. Even though, I usually sleep late at night, it still gives me the energy and support that I can use to fight minimal illness. 

love, elaine

Aica Bourne
