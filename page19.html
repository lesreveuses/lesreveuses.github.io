E N T R Y  N O .  7

"Finished a book today!"

    Nowadays, writing love letters through the traditional way, despite of the rising technology, may sound so old-fashioned however the main character here displayed how she enjoyed it more by writing down all the secrets and impressions in her letters and treasured like it is her most valuable possession. To All the Boys I’ve Loved Before, was written by Jenny Han who was also the author of The Summer I Turned Pretty. It was published by the Simon & Schuster Children’s Publishing Division last 2014 in 1230 Avenue of the Americas, New York, New York 10020. It consists 72 chapters and also can be considered as a fictional book. This book will let the reader to travel back towards ‘those’ teenage days that will put him or her into a roller-coaster emotion. 

     Lara Jean Covey was contented on her tranquilized and typical teenage life. She loves to write love letters for all the boys that she had loved before and keep it on a teal hatbox. Those letters were intended to be kept and secured for her eyes only but fate came in way. Lara Jean’s hatbox went missing and all the letters inside it were emailed to the recipients, leading her to the guy that she did not even anticipated on loving and little did she know that it will break her heart at the same time. 

     One of the many scenes that he or she should look forward was when Lara Jean and Peter attended their first party together. The reader who would be in this chapter would be so thrilled about the fact that Genevieve is really desperate pushing Lara Jean to spill all the beans about her boyfriend. Anyone would approve that Lara Jean and Peter succeeded on pretending that they are actually a one true pair. It was absolutely fun to imagine Gen’s jealous expression during the whole conversation. 

     Jenny Han, the author, made her story appealing to the readers in many ways. One of it is giving the characters a distinctive representation of the teenagers at present. Lara Jean portrayed as a girl who was already contented by having a sedated social life. On the other hand, Peter Kavinsky portrays the popular teenage boy in the town but has a broken family. Margot Covey displays as the type of sister who is not afraid of responsibilities and who treats her family always as her first priority and Josh as the boy next door that anyone can be smitten with. These are just some of the main characters that a reader would like.

     To All the Boys I’ve Loved Before would probably be best appreciated by the teenagers in the present. They would perhaps understand what Lara Jean is experiencing and what kind of emotions she is showing. This book will surely latch and keep someone in place because of wanting to read more.
